Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein was a brilliant physicist who is best known for his theory of relativity, which revolutionized our understanding of space, time, and energy. Born in 1879 in Germany, he was curious and imaginative from a young age. In 1905, often called his "miracle year," he published papers that introduced groundbreaking ideas, including E=mc², which shows that mass can be converted into energy.

Albert Einstein is born on March 14 in Ulm, Germany.

1880

The Einstein family moves to Munich.

1896

instein renounces his German citizenship to avoid military service and enrolls at the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich.

1900

Graduates with a diploma in mathematics and physics.

1905

1908

Begins lecturing at the University of Bern.

1909

Appointed associate professor at the University of Zurich.

1911

Becomes a full professor at the Karl-Ferdinand University in Prague.

1914

Moves to Berlin, becoming a member of the Prussian Academy of Sciences.

1915

Completes the general theory of relativity.

1919

The theory of general relativity is confirmed by observations during a solar eclipse.

1921

Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect.

1933

Moves to the United States to escape the rise of the Nazi regime in Germany. Takes a position at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey.

1939

Signs a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt, leading to the establishment of the Manhattan Project.

1940

Becomes a U.S. citizen while retaining his Swiss citizenship.

1952

Is offered the presidency of Israel but declines.

1955

Dies on April 18 in Princeton, New Jersey.
